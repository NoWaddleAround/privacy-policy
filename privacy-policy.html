Privacy Policy

Riftbound Database · Last updated: 17 July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the Riftbound Database app (package com.nowaddlearound.riftbounddatabase, the “App”) handles information. Riftbound Database is a free, non-commercial fan-made application for organising a personal collection of Riftbound trading cards. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by Riot Games, Inc.

In short: the App does not collect, transmit, or sell any personal data. It has no user accounts and no servers of its own. Everything you create stays on your device.

1. Information we do not collect

The App does not collect, store on our servers, or share any of the following:

  • Personal identifiers (name, email address, phone number, account IDs).
  • Location data.
  • Contacts, messages, or files unrelated to the App.
  • Analytics, usage statistics, or advertising identifiers.
  • Any data sold or shared with advertisers or data brokers.

The App contains no advertising, no third-party analytics, and no tracking SDKs.

2. Data stored on your device

All of the data you create in the App is stored locally on your device only (in the App’s private storage) and is never uploaded to us. This includes:

  • Your card collections, inventory quantities, and favorites.
  • Decks you build.
  • Your list of recently scanned or added cards.
  • App settings and preferences.
  • On-device text-recognition corrections used to improve the card scanner.

You remain in full control of this data. You can edit or delete it at any time from within the App, and uninstalling the App removes it from your device.

3. Camera and the card scanner

The App requests permission to use your device’s camera for a single purpose: to scan physical trading cards so their names can be recognised and added to your collection.

  • The camera feed is used only while the scanner screen is open.
  • Card images are processed entirely on your device using on-device text recognition (Google ML Kit) and image processing (OpenCV).
  • Camera images are not saved to a photo gallery, not uploaded, and not transmitted to us or any third party. Only the recognised card’s identity (e.g. its name and set) is stored, so it can be added to your local collection.

4. Import, export, and backups

  • CSV import / export and backup / restore read and write files only in locations you choose on your device. These files stay under your control and are not sent to us.
  • The App also keeps an automatic local safety copy of your collection in its own private storage on the device.
  • Android Auto Backup: because the App allows Android’s standard backup feature, Android may back up the App’s data (your collection and settings, roughly 1 MB) to your own Google Drive account and restore it when you reinstall the App or set up a new device. This is a feature of the Android operating system, controlled by your device and Google account settings, and is governed by Google’s Privacy Policy. You can disable it in your device’s Backup settings.

5. Links to external websites

The App contains links that open outside the App in your web browser. When you follow one of these links, you leave the App and the destination website’s own privacy policy applies:

  • Cardmarket — “buy” links that take you to a card’s listing or search page (cardmarket.com).
  • Ko-fi — an optional voluntary tip-jar link (ko-fi.com/blessofsoraka).
  • Official Riftbound rules, patch notes, and errata — links to public web pages.

The App does not share any of your data with these websites. Any information you provide to them (for example, if you choose to make a purchase or a voluntary tip) is handled solely by that website.

6. Google Play Services / ML Kit

The App uses Google’s ML Kit text-recognition library to read card names on your device. Depending on your device, the underlying recognition model may be provided through Google Play Services. Google’s handling of any data in that process is governed by Google’s Privacy Policy. The App itself does not send your card images or scan results to Google or anyone else.

7. Children’s privacy

The App does not collect personal information from anyone, including children. It is a general-audience collection-management tool and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any personal data from children.

8. Data security

Because your data never leaves your device (except through backups and exports that you initiate), the primary protection for your data is your own device’s security. The App stores its data in Android’s private per-app storage, which other apps cannot access.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated “Last updated” date. Continued use of the App after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
